What Is Soccer?

Soccer (known as football in most of the world) is the most widely played and watched sport on the planet. Two teams of 11 players each compete to score goals by getting a ball into the opposing team's net, using any part of the body except the arms and hands (except for the goalkeeper).

A standard match lasts 90 minutes, split into two halves of 45 minutes. The team that scores the most goals wins. If the game ends in a draw in a knockout competition, extra time and penalty kicks may be used to determine a winner.

Whether you want to play, watch, or simply understand the game better, this site will walk you through everything you need to know to get started.

The Objective of Soccer

The goal of soccer is simple: score more goals than your opponent during the 90-minute match. A goal is counted when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the two goalposts and under the crossbar.

While the objective is straightforward, the beauty of the game lies in the strategy, teamwork, and individual brilliance required to create and take goal-scoring opportunities ‚Äî all while defending against the opposing team doing the same.

The sport also has deep cultural importance across the globe. It promotes teamwork, physical fitness, discipline, and international understanding. With over 265 million players worldwide, soccer truly is the world's game.
